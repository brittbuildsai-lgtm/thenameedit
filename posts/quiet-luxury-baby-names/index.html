Frequently Asked Questions

Quiet luxury names are sophisticated without being flashy. They sound like they belong in a family with a trust fund and a summer home — think names like Bennett, Charlotte, Margaux, and Wells.

While old money names can be grand and aristocratic (Cordelia, Augustus), quiet luxury names are more restrained and modern. They're the naming equivalent of a $3,000 coat with no visible logo.

Consider names like Brooks, Grant, Reid, Hayes, Ellis, Pierce, and Vaughn — all short, strong, and effortlessly sophisticated.
