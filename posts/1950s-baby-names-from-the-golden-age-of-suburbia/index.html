Frequently Asked Questions

The 1950s favored names like Mary, Linda, Deborah, Susan, and Karen for girls, and James, Michael, Robert, David, and John for boys. Many have a clean, wholesome quality that defined the era.

Names like Donna, Sandra, and Gary still feel dated, but others like Linda, Judith, and Dennis are starting to sound fresh again. The cycle of name trends typically takes about 100 years for a full comeback.

The 1950s were influenced by Hollywood (Debbie Reynolds, James Dean), TV shows (I Love Lucy), and postwar optimism. The decade favored traditional, patriotic-sounding names.
