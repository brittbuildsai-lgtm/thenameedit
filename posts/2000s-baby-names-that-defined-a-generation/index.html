Frequently Asked Questions

The 2000s were dominated by Emily, Madison, Emma, Olivia, and Abigail for girls, and Jacob, Michael, Ethan, Joshua, and Matthew for boys. The -aiden/-ayden trend also exploded during this decade.

The 2000s brought us the -aiden explosion (Aiden, Brayden, Jayden, Hayden), the rise of Madison (thanks to Splash), and celebrity-inspired names. Reality TV and social media began influencing name choices.

They're in the 'awkward middle' phase — too recent to feel vintage but old enough to feel dated. Give it another 10-20 years and names like Madison, Kaylee, and Brody will likely cycle back with fresh appeal.
