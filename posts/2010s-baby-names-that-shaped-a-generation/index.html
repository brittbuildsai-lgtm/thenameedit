2010s Baby Names That Shaped a Generation

The 2010s were the decade of Instagram, avocado toast, and some of the most beautifully curated baby names in history. This was the era where parents stopped naming kids after relatives and started naming them like main characters. Vintage revivals, nature names, and strong-but-soft picks dominated — and honestly? The taste was impeccable.

Frequently Asked Questions

Emma and Liam dominated the decade, holding the #1 spots for years. Other chart-toppers included Olivia, Sophia, Noah, and Oliver. The 2010s also saw the rise of names like Luna, Aria, and Declan.

The 2010s brought vintage revivals (Eleanor, Theodore, Hazel), nature names (Willow, Jasper, Ivy), short punchy names (Kai, Leo, Mia), and literary/pop culture influences (Aria from Game of Thrones, Elsa from Frozen).

The 2010s moved away from the -aiden trend and embraced more classic, vintage-inspired names. Parents traded Jayden and Brayden for Theodore and Oliver. The overall aesthetic shifted from trendy to timeless.
